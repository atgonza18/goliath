# OAC Meeting Questions — February 26, 2026

**Prepared for:** Owner's Representative / Project Controls
**Purpose:** Schedule accountability and constraint discovery across four active solar projects
**Portfolio Context:** Two White Construction/MasTec projects in severe schedule distress; two Wanzek/MasTec projects in early stages with emerging slippage

---

## BLACKFORD SOLAR (211 MW DC) — White Construction / MasTec

**Status Summary:** Overall float at -53 days. Backfeed/interconnect -94 days behind contract. All 8 circuit MCs behind contract dates. Substation procurement critically late. Array and pile work still has months of duration remaining.

### Schedule Accountability Questions

1. **Backfeed is now forecast for June 1, 2026 — 133 days past the January 19 contract date.** What is your written recovery plan to compress this timeline, and when will you submit it for review?

2. **1st Circuit MC is sitting at -79 days against the November 20, 2025 contract date, with a forecast of March 25, 2026.** You've already blown this milestone by over four months. What specific crew additions or shift changes have you implemented since this milestone was missed, and what evidence do we have that March 25 is credible?

3. **All eight circuit MCs are behind their contract dates — not one is on track.** At what point did your project team recognize that every circuit was slipping, and why wasn't this escalated to the Owner earlier?

4. **SWPPP is at -143 days of float.** Explain how you are maintaining environmental compliance with SWPPP this far behind, and has the site received any notices of violation or regulatory inquiries?

5. **Substation equipment install is at -88 days of float.** Walk us through the substation installation sequence from today to energization — what are the remaining activities, their durations, and the crews assigned to each?

6. **Substation steel is at -89 float, insulators at -77, and MPT at -65 — all still outstanding procurement.** Provide confirmed shipping dates with tracking numbers for each of these items. When do they physically arrive on site?

7. **Array construction still has 67 days of remaining duration.** At current installation rates, what is the actual date you project array completion? Show us the production curve — are you accelerating, decelerating, or flat?

8. **Pile foundations have 21 days of remaining duration, but every downstream activity is already massively negative.** Even if piles finish on schedule, how does that help when racking and module installation are dependent on pile completion that should have happened months ago?

9. **What is your current daily/weekly module installation rate versus what the baseline schedule assumed?** Provide actual production data for the last four weeks compared to planned production.

10. **You are the general contractor on both Blackford and Duff, and both projects are in severe distress.** Has White Construction/MasTec leadership reviewed these two projects together and made a corporate-level decision about resource allocation? We need a direct answer.

### Constraint Discovery Questions

1. **What material deliveries in the next 60 days are you concerned about that are NOT currently reflected in your procurement log?** Specifically — combiner boxes, wire, connectors, tracker components — are any of these at risk?

2. **Walk me through your crew plan for the next 30 days.** How many pile crews, racking crews, and module crews are on site today versus what the recovery schedule requires? Where are the gaps?

3. **Are you experiencing any subcontractor performance issues that you haven't formally reported?** Specifically, are any subs threatening to demobilize, demanding change orders, or failing to meet weekly targets?

4. **What is the status of your interconnection agreement and utility coordination?** Has the utility been notified of the revised backfeed date? Are there any utility outage windows or seasonal restrictions that could further delay backfeed beyond June 1?

5. **What weather days have you lost in the last 60 days, and how does that compare to what the schedule assumed?** Are you carrying adequate weather contingency for the spring rain season?

6. **Is there any pending or unresolved design issue — civil, electrical, structural — that is blocking work in any area of the site?** RFIs that are overdue, design changes not yet incorporated, IFC drawing revisions?

7. **What is the status of your testing and commissioning plan?** With 8 circuits all behind, have you pre-positioned commissioning resources, or are you planning to figure that out later? What is your planned sequence for circuit energization?

8. **Are there any land access, easement, or right-of-way issues affecting any portion of the site?** Any areas where you cannot work due to environmental buffers, cultural resources, or landowner disputes?

9. **What is the current status of your QA/QC program?** Are you seeing rework or punchlist rates that are higher than planned? What is your torque audit pass rate on tracker installations?

10. **Have you had any safety incidents, near-misses, or OSHA interactions in the last 30 days that could affect your ability to maintain production?** Any stop-work orders or stand-downs?

## DUFF SOLAR (138 MW DC) — White Construction / MasTec

**Status Summary:** Worst-performing project in portfolio at -158 days on circuit completions. Paradoxically, the HV/substation is complete and was energized January 8, 2026 — the bottleneck is entirely in LV solar field construction. Modules have 78 days remaining, racking 81 days, piles 28 days. The energized substation is sitting idle waiting for circuits.

### Schedule Accountability Questions

1. **The substation has been energized since January 8, 2026, and you have zero circuits complete — the substation is literally sitting idle.** How much is each day of delay costing in lost revenue opportunity, and what is White Construction's plan to address the liquidated damages exposure?

2. **1st Circuit MC is at -117 days — contract date was October 23, 2025, and you're now forecasting April 14, 2026.** That is a six-month delay on a milestone that the schedule said would be done last fall. When did this milestone first appear at risk in your reporting, and what actions were taken at that time?

3. **3rd Circuit MC is at -158 days — the worst float number in the entire portfolio.** Break down for us exactly what is on the critical path to 3rd Circuit MC and the remaining duration for each activity.

4. **Module placement has 78 days remaining with -158 float.** What is your current module installation rate in MW per day? What rate does the recovery schedule require? Show us the delta.

5. **Racking has 81 days remaining with -152 float — meaning racking is actually a longer-duration activity than modules right now.** Why does racking have MORE remaining duration than module placement? Is racking the true bottleneck, not modules?

6. **Pile installation has 28 days remaining with float ranging from -100 to -124 across different areas.** You still have nearly a month of pile driving left on a project where circuits should have been complete last October. What is the daily pile count versus plan?

7. **Every single LV solar field activity is massively behind while the HV side was delivered successfully.** Was this a deliberate sequencing decision, a resource allocation failure, or a subcontractor issue? Who made the call to prioritize HV over LV field work?

8. **Given the -158 day position, has White Construction submitted a formal recovery schedule for Owner review?** If yes, when was it submitted and what acceleration measures does it include? If no, why not?

9. **What are the contractual milestone dates that trigger liquidated damages, and which have already been passed?** Provide a clear table showing each LD milestone, the contract date, the current forecast, and the number of days exposed.

10. **At the current burn rate on the LV field, what is the realistic full project completion date — not the optimistic schedule date, but the date your superintendent would bet his own money on?**

### Constraint Discovery Questions

1. **Your substation is energized and your field is empty — this is a highly unusual situation.** Are there any arc flash, safety zone, or energized work boundary issues that are complicating field work near the energized substation?

2. **What is the labor situation on site right now?** How many total craft workers are on site today? How many were on site one month ago? How many does your recovery plan require? Where are they coming from?

3. **Are you sharing ANY craft labor, equipment, or supervision between Duff and Blackford?** If a crew finishes at Duff, is it going to Blackford or vice versa? We need complete transparency on resource sharing.

4. **What is your module inventory position?** How many modules are on site, how many are in transit, and what is the remaining delivery schedule? Is there any risk of a module supply gap that would idle your installation crews?

5. **Are there any tracker or racking component shortages?** Specifically — torque tubes, motors, bearings, clamps, purlins — are any of these backordered or arriving late?

6. **What electrical termination and testing work is required between module installation and circuit energization?** How long is that tail, and have you mobilized the electrical subs to be ready to work immediately behind the module crews?

7. **Is soil condition, water table, or any geotechnical issue affecting your pile installation rate?** Are you encountering refusal, running into rock, or dealing with saturated soils that are slowing you down?

8. **What is the state of your string inverter or central inverter installations?** Are inverters on site? Installed? Tested? Or is this another procurement/installation activity that is sitting in the queue?

9. **Are there any outstanding change orders, claims, or disputes that are affecting your willingness or ability to accelerate?** Is there a commercial dispute that is causing White Construction to slow-walk this project?

10. **What is your warranty and turnover plan?** With the project this far behind, have you thought through how you're going to manage warranty start dates, O&M transition, and as-built documentation — or is that all being deferred?

## PECAN PRAIRIE NORTH (407 MW DC) — Wanzek / MasTec

**Status Summary:** Early-stage project in civil/clearing phase. Already projecting approximately 84 days of slippage on major milestones. MC 250 MW slipping from December 31, 2026 to March 5, 2027. Full MC slipping from February 19, 2027 to May 13, 2027. This is a 407 MW project — the largest in the portfolio — and it is starting to slip before major construction even begins.

### Schedule Accountability Questions

1. **You are projecting 84 days of slippage on MC 250 MW and the project is still in the clearing phase.** If you're already 84 days behind before you've driven a single pile, what gives us confidence you won't be 200 days behind when you're in full production?

2. **MC 250 MW has moved from December 31, 2026 to March 5, 2027.** What is the root cause of this 64-day slip? Is it a late start, slower-than-planned clearing, or something else entirely?

3. **Full MC has slipped from February 19, 2027 to May 13, 2027 — 83 days.** Break down the critical path from today to Full MC. Which activity is driving the slip — is it civil, procurement, or an upstream dependency?

4. **Tree removal still has 45 days remaining.** What is your current tree removal daily production versus planned? How many acres have been cleared to date versus the total scope?

5. **Mass grading is not scheduled to start until April 2, 2026 — over a month away.** Is this date firm? What are the predecessors, and are any of them at risk of pushing mass grading even later?

6. **Module delivery is scheduled to start April 17, 2026.** Have purchase orders been placed? Are manufacturing slots confirmed? What is the delivery cadence (MW per week), and does it align with your installation plan?

7. **This is a 407 MW project — the largest in the portfolio.** What is your peak crew plan? How many pile drivers, racking crews, and module installation crews at peak? When does peak mobilization occur?

8. **At 84 days of projected slippage, have you developed a mitigation or recovery plan?** If the Owner demanded that you hold the December 31, 2026 MC 250 MW date, what specific acceleration measures would you propose and what would they cost?

9. **What are the key contract milestones that carry liquidated damages, and what is the daily LD rate?** At 84 days of slip, what is the projected LD exposure if no recovery actions are taken?

10. **Are there any permitting, environmental, or regulatory approvals still outstanding that could further delay construction?** Specifically — stormwater permits, grading permits, county road permits, T&E species clearances?

### Constraint Discovery Questions

1. **Walk me through the site access situation.** How many access points do you have? Are county roads adequate for heavy equipment and module deliveries? Any road improvement or bonding requirements?

2. **What is the geotechnical profile of this site?** Have you completed your geotech investigation? Are there any areas with rock, expansive clay, high water table, or other conditions that could affect pile driving rates?

3. **At 407 MW, this project will require an enormous volume of materials — piles, trackers, modules, wire, inverters, transformers.** Walk us through your procurement status for each major material category. What is ordered, what is on site, what is at risk?

4. **What is your laydown and staging plan for a project this size?** Do you have adequate laydown area for module storage? How are you planning material logistics to avoid double-handling?

5. **What is the local labor market like for this project?** Where are your craft workers coming from? Are you using local hire, traveling crews, or a mix? What is the housing/lodging situation — is it constraining your ability to bring in crews?

6. **Are there any adjacent landowner issues, noise restrictions, or work-hour limitations that could affect your production rates?** Any cultural resource areas, wetland buffers, or setback requirements within the site?

7. **What is your weather contingency plan?** What region is this in, and what is the historical weather pattern during your peak construction months? How many weather days are built into the schedule?

8. **Are there any transmission or interconnection scope items that Wanzek is responsible for?** What is the status of the gen-tie line, switchyard, or POI work?

9. **What lessons learned from other Wanzek solar projects are you applying here?** Specifically, what went wrong on past projects that you are actively mitigating on Pecan Prairie North?

10. **Is there any shared infrastructure, access, or sequencing dependency between Pecan Prairie North and Pecan Prairie South?** If one project slips, does it affect the other?

## PECAN PRAIRIE SOUTH (188 MW DC) — Wanzek / MasTec

**Status Summary:** Early-stage project with manageable slippage. Mobilization delayed by approximately 154 days (contract September 29, 2025 to forecast March 2, 2026). T-Line energization showing ~51 days of slip. MC/SC dates only ~10 days behind — but this assumes the current slippage doesn't compound.

### Schedule Accountability Questions

1. **Mobilization was contracted for September 29, 2025 and is now forecast for March 2, 2026 — a 154-day delay.** What caused a five-month delay in mobilization? Was this an Owner-directed delay, a permitting issue, or a contractor failure to mobilize?

2. **Despite the massive mobilization delay, MC/SC dates are only showing ~10 days of slip.** How is that mathematically possible? Either the original schedule had enormous float built in, or the current forecast is not credible. Explain the logic.

3. **T-Line energization is showing approximately 51 days of slip.** What is the T-Line scope, who is performing the work, and what is driving the 51-day delay? Is this a Wanzek scope item or a utility/third-party dependency?

4. **Tree removal is scheduled to start April 20, 2026.** Is this date firm? What is the scope — how many acres of clearing, and what is the planned duration?

5. **Given that you haven't mobilized yet, when do you project having boots on the ground and equipment rolling?** Provide a detailed ramp-up plan from March 2 mobilization through first pile driven.

6. **What is the current schedule basis — is this a baseline schedule, a re-baseline, or an update to the original?** If the schedule has been re-baselined to absorb the mobilization delay, when was that approved by the Owner?

7. **The fact that MC/SC dates only show ~10 days of slip after a 154-day mobilization delay suggests significant schedule compression.** What acceleration measures are baked into the current forecast — overtime, additional crews, extended shifts, weekend work?

8. **What are the key intermediate milestones between now and MC?** Provide a milestone summary showing: clearing complete, grading complete, first pile, first module, first circuit MC, 50% MC, full MC — with contract dates and current forecast dates for each.

9. **If tree removal doesn't start until April 20, when does pile driving start?** Walk us through the civil-to-structural handoff timeline. How long after clearing and grading do you project first pile?

10. **At 188 MW, this is less than half the size of Pecan Prairie North.** Are you using the same crews, or does each project have dedicated resources? What is the resource-sharing plan between North and South?

### Constraint Discovery Questions

1. **With mobilization 154 days late, what has changed on site since September 2025?** Have site conditions changed — vegetation regrowth, erosion, stormwater issues — that will add scope to your clearing and civil work?

2. **What permits and approvals are in hand versus outstanding?** Specifically — grading permit, building permit, electrical permit, stormwater permit, road use agreement, utility coordination agreements?

3. **What is your module procurement status?** Are modules ordered? Is the delivery schedule confirmed? When does the first truck arrive, and does that align with your installation plan?

4. **Are there any easement, right-of-way, or land access issues that contributed to the mobilization delay or that could affect future work?** Any legal or title issues still being resolved?

5. **What is the interconnection timeline from the utility's perspective?** Have you confirmed the utility's readiness date for interconnection? Is there a queue position or system upgrade that could become a constraint?

6. **What subcontractors are you planning to use, and are their contracts executed?** Specifically — pile driving, electrical, commissioning — are these subs mobilization-ready, or are you still in procurement?

7. **Is there a specific weather or seasonal window that you are targeting or concerned about?** For example, is there a window where you MUST complete grading before monsoon season, or a thermal limit on module installation?

8. **What is the relationship between the T-Line work and the rest of the project?** Is the T-Line on a separate critical path, and could a 51-day T-Line delay ultimately become the controlling constraint regardless of field progress?

9. **Given the late mobilization, have you reassessed your construction execution plan?** Are you planning any changes to sequencing, phasing, or resource loading compared to the original bid plan?

10. **What is your single biggest concern about this project that you haven't put in writing yet?** If there's something keeping your project manager up at night, we'd rather hear it now than in six months.

## CROSS-PORTFOLIO QUESTIONS

These questions address systemic issues across the four projects and should be directed at senior MasTec/White Construction/Wanzek leadership.

### White Construction / MasTec Corporate Questions

1. **Both White Construction projects — Blackford and Duff — are in severe schedule distress, with Duff at -158 days and Blackford at -94 days on interconnect.** Is this a company-wide resource crisis, or is there a specific operational issue affecting these two projects?

2. **What is the crew-sharing arrangement between Blackford and Duff?** Are pile drivers, racking crews, or module installers being moved between sites? If one project is being prioritized over the other, which one and why?

3. **Has White Construction/MasTec senior leadership — VP level or above — personally reviewed these two projects in the last 30 days?** If so, what corporate commitments were made, and when will we see results on the ground?

4. **What is MasTec's current backlog and how are Blackford and Duff prioritized within the broader MasTec/White Construction portfolio?** Are resources being pulled from our projects to support other clients?

5. **At what point does the Owner need to consider supplementing White Construction's workforce with third-party crews?** Is that conversation happening internally at MasTec, or does the Owner need to initiate it?

### Wanzek / MasTec Corporate Questions

6. **Both Pecan Prairie projects are in early stages but already showing slippage.** Given what we're seeing on the White Construction side of the MasTec family, what assurances can Wanzek provide that these projects won't follow the same trajectory?

7. **Pecan Prairie North is a 407 MW project — nearly the scale of a utility-scale gas plant.** Does Wanzek have experience executing a project of this size in solar? What is the largest solar project Wanzek has completed, and how does its workforce compare to what North requires?

8. **Are Pecan Prairie North and South being managed by the same project team, or do they have independent PMs, superintendents, and schedulers?** What is the org chart?

### Portfolio-Level Strategic Questions

9. **Three of four projects are MasTec entities (White Construction and Wanzek are both MasTec subsidiaries).** What is the consolidated MasTec resource commitment to this portfolio? Is there a single MasTec executive accountable for all three engagements?

10. **If we rank these four projects by distress level, it's Duff > Blackford > Pecan North > Pecan South.** Given finite corporate resources, what is MasTec's triage strategy? Are they writing off schedule recovery on Duff and focusing on Blackford?

11. **Across the portfolio, what is the aggregate LD exposure at current forecast dates?** We need a consolidated view — not project-by-project optimism, but a realistic financial exposure number.

12. **For the two projects in late-stage distress (Duff and Blackford), is it time to engage an independent scheduling consultant to validate the contractor's forecasts?** The current forecast dates have low credibility given the track record of repeated slippage.

13. **What is the commissioning resource plan across the portfolio?** With Duff and Blackford both pushing into the same timeframe, and Pecan North's MC now in early 2027, are we going to have commissioning resource conflicts?

14. **Are there any force majeure, excusable delay, or change order claims pending on ANY of these four projects that could affect the schedule analysis?** We need a complete picture of the commercial landscape alongside the schedule data.

15. **What is the Owner's escalation protocol from this point forward?** Given the severity of the White Construction situation, should we be moving to weekly OAC calls, requiring daily production reports, or placing Owner's representatives on site full-time?
